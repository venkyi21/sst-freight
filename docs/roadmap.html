04

US / Japan customs — how much harder, really

Meaningfully harder than Week 10's ICEGATE work, and mostly for regulatory reasons, not engineering ones.

Week 10 is viable because SST Freight already operates (or is building toward operating) as a licensed CHA in India with ICEGATE access. That foundation doesn't exist for the US or Japan — it's a new regulatory relationship, not a new screen.

  • US filing (ABI) generally requires a licensed US Customs Broker and a customs bond — a real financial surety instrument, not a feature toggle.
  • US alone splits into three separate systems — AES (export declarations), ISF/"10+2" (import security filing, due 24h before vessel loading), and AMS/ABI (entry filing) — each with its own CBP certification cycle, typically months long.
  • Japan's AFR via NACCS is a fourth, entirely separate proprietary system, generally requiring local presence or a Japan-based partner.
  • None of this is one-time — bonds and licenses need renewing, regulations change. It's an ongoing operational/legal commitment, not a shipped feature.

The real scope decision

Option A

Become a certified filer

  • SST Freight itself files AES/ISF/ABI/AFR for customers
  • Requires licensing, bonding, multi-system CBP/NACCS certification
  • Likely 6–12+ months, new hires or broker partnerships
Difficulty: this is a different business, not a feature
Option B

Integrate with existing filers

  • Customers coordinate with a US/Japan-side broker who already files
  • SST Freight exchanges status/data with that broker's system for visibility only
  • Shape of work is close to Week 9's carrier EDI integration
Difficulty: tractable — a future roadmap item, not a new business
If the goal is matching GoFreight's filing claim directly, that's Option A. If the goal is "our Indian customers can see US-side customs status without leaving our platform," that's Option B. Worth deciding which one is meant before either goes on a future version of this roadmap.
05

Not actually a gap

One thing worth naming so it doesn't get mistaken for missing work.

US/Japan-specific customs systems are not a gap for SST Freight's current positioning. The platform is built around Indian ports and Indian customs terminology (Bill of Entry / Shipping Bill, CHA workflow) — that's a different regulatory market than GoFreight's AES/ISF/AFR JP24 support, not a feature you're missing for your actual target customer.
