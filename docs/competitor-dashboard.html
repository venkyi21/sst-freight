§ 02 — Thesis under this lens
The bet
Buyout economics + India-compliance moat = a category of one.
The freight-tech market has SaaS players and legacy players. Nobody has monetized the on-prem/perpetual model for modern architecture in India. That gap is the wedge, and SCMTR compliance is the reason forwarders will pay for it.
The counter-risk
One-time revenue vs recurring — the multiples question.
Investors will ask how buyout revenue compares to SaaS ARR on multiples. Answer: flat AMC (18% of license/yr) + Docker support + integration services stabilize recurring at ~40% of top-line by year 3.
The bet
You buy it once. You own it. It speaks Indian customs.
Freightify and Fresa charge you per seat, forever. Eroyal makes you install Windows software. CargoWise costs more than your revenue. SST is a Docker container you own, a modern UI your junior clerks actually enjoy, and a SCMTR simulator that stops bill errors before they cost you penalties.
The counter-risk
You are betting on a young vendor. Here is how we de-risk that.
You get source-code escrow, a 90-day money-back pilot, an on-site engineer for the first month, and a public case-study clause. If we disappear, you still own the Docker image and the database.
The bet
Ship the moat before the parity. Compliance and buyout first.
We will lose bake-offs on feature-count for another 6 months no matter what. So do not chase Fresa on modules. Chase the two things that make forwarders switch: SCMTR simulator (week 9) and Docker/RLS story (weeks 1-3). Everything else is second.
The counter-risk
What we are explicitly not building this quarter.
No air-freight consol tooling, no CRM, no accounting GL beyond what the freight ledger needs, no mobile-first driver app. Every one of these is a rabbit hole that competitors already own. Say no.

Option 2 — why reframing wins for a buyout-model bootstrap
01
Keeps the runway free
The whole 10-year TCO pitch depends on near-zero, predictable fixed costs during early validation. A sales-gated, quote-only path to global coverage today's users don't need yet is the opposite of that.
02
Matches real trade lanes
Chennai/Andhra Pradesh CHAs move 80%+ of cargo through a handful of major lines (Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd). Chasing Freightify's 30+ global feeds is coverage nobody local is asking for.
03
Leads with real strengths
Buyout clients aren't buying feed count — they're escaping the SaaS headcount tax. The ICEGATE simulator, RLS isolation, and Meter-Flip margin tracking already outscore competitors on the axes that matter.
